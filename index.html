    <h1>Course Description</h1>

    <div class="article-wrap">

      <p style="text-align:justify">This course provides a broad understanding of applications, technologies, and processes for gathering, storing,

      accessing, and analyzing data to help business users make better decisions.

     

  Topics include

        <a href="https://www.google.com/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=&esrc=s&source=web&cd=&cad=rja&uact=8&ved=2ahUKEwiS04qSnLTrAhWCdt8KHV8KDMYQFjADegQIAhAB&url=https%3A%2F%2Faisel.aisnet.org%2Fmisq%2Fvol41%2Fiss2%2F2%2F&usg=AOvVaw2Dg1bvEBZlmwdP2NWXTsSl" target="_blank">problem formulation</a>, and

  <a href="https://r4ds.had.co.nz/introduction.html#r" target="_blank">research design</a> and

      <a href="https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/next-chapter-analytics-data-storytelling" target="_blank">story telling with data</a>;

      <a href="https://r4ds.had.co.nz/introduction.html#r" target="_blank">R</a>, 

  <a href= "https://r4ds.had.co.nz/introduction.html#rstudio" target="_blank">RStudio</a>,  

  <a href="https://r4ds.had.co.nz/r-markdown.html" target="_blank"> R Markdown</a>, and

      <a href="https://www.tableau.com" target="_blank"> Tableau</a>; 

  <a href= "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8EMW7io4rSI" target="_blank">visualization</a>,   

  <a href= "https://r4ds.had.co.nz/transform.html" target="_blank">transformation</a>, and

      <a href= "https://r4ds.had.co.nz/wrangle-intro.html" target="_blank">wrangling</a>;

      <a href= "https://r4ds.had.co.nz/model-intro.html" target="_blank">modeling</a>, 

      <a href= "https://stat4701.github.io/edav/2015/04/02/rvest_tutorial/" target="_blank">webscraping</a>,

      <a href= "http://varianceexplained.org/r/yelp-sentiment/" target="_blank">sentiment analysis</a>,  and 

      <a href= "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PPLop4L2eGk" target="_blank">machine learning</a>.

  These topics are divided into the following sections: (1) asking meaningful questions, (2) using rigorous methods, (3) developing useful solutions.</p>

  <p><br></p>

   

    <p style="text-align:justify"> 

    This course uses the <b>Hybrid Synchronous</b> instructional approach. 

    Under this approach, a certain number of students (based on classroom capacity) join the instructor on a rotating basis for socially-distanced,

    face-to-face instruction during each class session, with remaining students joining synchronously through Zoom during the assigned class time. 

    Students are encouraged to attend class in-person on their designated days. 

    Information about your assigned cohort (Tuesday or Thursday) is available in the Welcome Module on eLC. 

</p>

         

    <p><br></p>

  <p style="text-align:justify"><i>Please note that the instructional approach may change as need warrants.</i></p>

    </p>

    <p><br></p>

     

     

    <h1>Pre-requisite</h1>

    <div class="article-wrap">

      <p style="text-align:justify">MIST 4610 and (BUSN 4000 or BUSN 4000E) or permission of department.</i></p>

 

    <p><br></p>

  <p style="text-align:justify"><i>The syllabus is a general plan for the course; deviations announced by the instructor may be necessary.</i></p>

<p><br></p>

<h1>Objectives</h1>

    <div class="article-wrap">

      <p style="text-align:justify"> Students completing this course will be able to: 

 

  <ul style="list-style-type:disc;">

 

  <li>Define and articulate problems worthy of investigation</li>

  <li>Match meaningful problems to suitable designs</li>  

  <li>Collect, explore, wrangle, and model data in R and RStudio</li>

  <li>Become familiar with Tableau for data science</li>

  <li>Understand the basics of machine learning</li>

  <li>Use storytelling to present data insights</li>

    

</ul>

</p>

  <br>

<h1>Text Book</h1>

    <div class="article-wrap">

      <p style="text-align:justify">Wickham, H., & Grolemund, G. (2017). <a href="https://r4ds.had.co.nz/" target="_blank"> R for Data Science: O’Reilly</a>.</p>

    <p><br></p>

    <p style="text-align:justify">Knaflic, C. G. (2015).<a href="https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1119002257/ref=ox_sc_act_title_2?smid=ATVPDKIKX0DER&psc=1" target="_blank"> Storytelling with Data</a>.</p>

    <p><br></p>

    <p style="text-align:justify">Ng, A. (2016). <a href="https://www.deeplearning.ai/machine-learning-yearning/" target="_blank"> Machine Learning Yearning</a>. </p>

  <p><br></p>

  <p style="text-align:justify">Provost, F. & Fawcett, T. (2013). <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Data-Science-Business-Data-Analytic-Thinking/dp/1449361323" target="_blank"> Data Science for Business</a>. </p>

  <p><br></p>

   

  <h1>Software</h1>

    <div class="article-wrap">

      <p style="text-align:justify"><a href="https://cloud.r-project.org" target = "_blank">R</a> and <a href="https://rstudio.com/products/rstudio/download/" target = "_blank">RStudio</a> for data exploring, wrangling, and modeling.

      Choose the <b>open-source desktop</b> version of RStudio.

      <a href="https://www.tableau.com/academic/students">Tableau</a> for visualization. Activate the product for one year (use <i>uga.edu</i> e-mail).

    </p>

<p><br></p>    

 

<h1>Attendance</h1>

    <div class="article-wrap">

      <p style="text-align:justify">Attendance is not required for this course but highly encouraged. A recent PNAS <a href="https://www.pnas.org/content/116/39/19251" target = "_blank">study</a> shows that &quot;<i>being actively engaged in the classroom</i>&quot; is a significant predictor of actual learning.</p>

<p><br></p>  

   

    <h1>File Security</h1>

    <div class="article-wrap">

      <p style="text-align:justify">You are responsible for the security of your own files. 

      To avoid problems, I recommend to regularly backup copies of your work.

    In addition, when modeling or visualizing data in R, save your files to prevent data loss in the case of a computer crash.</p>

  <p><br></p>

      <p style="text-align:justify">As a University of Georgia student, you have agreed to abide by the University's academic honesty policy, &quot;A Culture of Honesty, &quot;

<p><br></p>

 

  <h1>Team Work</h1>

    <div class="article-wrap">

      <p style="text-align:justify">In this course, you will work in groups. As a result, review a short report on <a href="https://carolina-lima-salge.github.io/mist4610/team_effectiveness.pdf" target="_blank">team effectiveness</a> and establish a team agreement (<a href="https://carolina-lima-salge.github.io/mist4610/sample_team_agreement.docx" target="_blank">sample agreement</a>) for use by the team.

    It occasionally happens that someone in a team is not prepared to do his/her share. In my courses, I recommend that the team give the freeloader one warning and then fire that person.

    That person will then do group assignments individually or find a new team to join. The instructor should be notified of changes in team composition immediately.

      <b>All teams should have about 3-5 members</b>.</p>

  <p><br></p>

    <h1>COVID-19 Information</h1>

    <div class="article-wrap">

      <p style="text-align:justify">

      <b>What do I do if I have symptoms?</b> Students showing symptoms should self-isolate and schedule an appointment with the University Health Center by calling 706-542-1162 (Monday-Friday, 8 a.m.-5 p.m.). 

      Please DO NOT walk-in. For emergencies and after-hours care, see <a href="https://www.uhs.uga.edu/info/emergencies" target="_blank"><u>https://www.uhs.uga.edu/info/emergencies</u></a>.

      

      </p>

    <p><br></p>

    <p style="text-align:justify">

<b>What do I do if I am notified that I have been exposed? </b>

      Students who learn they have been directly exposed to COVID-19 but are not showing symptoms should self-quarantine for 14 days consistent with Department of Public Health (DPH) and Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) guidelines. 

      Please correspond with your instructor via <a href="mailto:calsalge@uga.edu" target="_blank">email</a>, with a cc: to Student Care & Outreach at sco@uga.edu, to coordinate continuing your coursework while self-quarantined. 

      If you develop symptoms, you should contact the University Health Center to make an appointment to be tested. You should continue to monitor your symptoms daily on DawgCheck.

      

      </p>

    <p><br></p>

    <p style="text-align:justify">

<b>How do I get a test?</b>  Students who are demonstrating symptoms of COVID-19 should call the University Health Center. 

UHC is offering testing by appointment for students; appointments may be booked by calling 706-542-1162. 

</p>

    <p><br></p>

    <p style="text-align:justify">

    UGA will also be recruiting asymptomatic students to participate in surveillance tests. Students living in residence halls, Greek housing and off-campus apartment complexes are encouraged to participate. 

    </p>

    <p><br></p>

    <p style="text-align:justify">

<b>What do I do if I test positive?</b>  

Any student with a positive COVID-19 test is required to report the test in DawgCheck and should self-isolate immediately. 

Students should not attend classes in-person until the isolation period is completed. 

Once you report the positive test through DawgCheck, UGA Student Care and Outreach will follow up with you. </p>
